Bleeding

Bleeding refers to the loss of blood from blood vessels anywhere in the body. If someone has been wounded and is bleeding, it is important to work quickly to control blood loss. In most cases, you should be able to keep the bleeding under control without much difficulty. In more severe cases, however, uncontrolled or severe bleeding can contribute to shock, circulatory disruption, or more serious health consequences. In some cases, uncontrolled bleeding may cause damage to tissues and major organs, which can lead to death. Assess the source and seriousness of the bleeding, and take appropriate first aid measures. For severe or uncontrolled bleeding, seek medical attention immediately.
